What I Strive Towards in Teaching

Good teaching, to me, means making ideas both rigorous and relevant. In courses on data science, machine learning, and AI, I try to go beyond conveying concepts — I want students to actually care about them. That means connecting material to real problems, building in hands-on projects, and leaving room for students to bring their own curiosity into the work.

I also think a lot about who's in the room. Technical spaces can feel unwelcoming to people who don't see themselves reflected in them, and I take that seriously. I try to run a classroom where questions are genuinely welcome, where struggle is normalized, and where every student feels like they belong. When students feel seen and supported, they learn better — and they start to see themselves as capable of contributing to the field.

Student Feedback

Every semester, my students are polled to rate the following:

  1. Overall rating of the Teaching Assistant
  2. The Teaching Assistant's ability to answer student questions
  3. The Teaching Assistant's ability to explain material and assist with any other aspects of the course and/or lab

I am proud to share that I average a score above 4.5/5 in all three categories.
