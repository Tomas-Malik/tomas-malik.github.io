## About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Chicago, working at the intersection of natural language processing and efficient large language model (LLM) systems.

My research focuses on improving reasoning efficiency in LLMs, particularly through routing strategies, inference-time methods, and cost–accuracy trade-offs. I am interested in how we can allocate computation more intelligently to achieve strong performance without the full cost of large models.

My recent work explores training-free routing mechanisms, including agreement-based approaches that dynamically distribute queries across models of different sizes. The goal is to approach state-of-the-art performance while significantly reducing inference cost—an increasingly important challenge as LLMs continue to scale.

Beyond research, I am actively involved in teaching and curriculum development. I have co-developed and taught applied data science and AI courses through Chicago Tech Circle, focusing on making machine learning tools accessible to students from diverse backgrounds. I enjoy designing hands-on projects that connect theory to real-world applications, including building AI agents and working with real datasets.

More broadly, I am interested in building practical, efficient, and interpretable AI systems. I am especially drawn to problems at the boundary of research and deployment, where theoretical ideas translate into measurable real-world impact.
